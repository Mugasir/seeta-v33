Schools overview

Browse our schools to find a path that fits your goals. Each school supports learning through focused teaching, supervised practice, and structured progression.

School of Computing and Technology

This school focuses on modern computing skills built through structured practice and project work. Programs include software engineering, cybersecurity, data science, and AI. Students learn through labs and guided hands-on activities that mirror industry workflows. The school supports career readiness through practical assignments, teamwork, and partnerships that expose learners to real expectations in technology environments.

School of Law

The School of Law provides breadth in legal education while keeping training grounded in practice. Students engage with core areas of law and develop skills in reasoning, writing, and advocacy. Practical exposure runs through moot courts, legal clinics, and internship pathways, supporting professional confidence and a clear understanding of legal service in real contexts.

School of Health Sciences

This school supports health-focused training with attention to competence and responsible practice. Programs include pharmacy and nursing, alongside public health related disciplines. Clinical experience and supervised learning help students build confidence in real service settings. The approach emphasizes skill, ethics, and a clear understanding of care within community health needs.

School of Business and Management

The School of Business and Management prepares students for professional performance through applied learning and structured progression. Areas include accounting, finance, marketing, HR, procurement, economics, and administration. Coursework links concepts to practical tasks such as planning, reporting, communication, and decision-making. Students graduate with stronger readiness for business environments and continued professional development.
